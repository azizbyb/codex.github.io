WEB DEVELOPMENT PROCESS

Know More About How We Create

Information Gathering The initial step involves gathering project information, analyzing business requirements, defining website purpose, and setting goals, with common objectives being information sharing and financial gain, to help the design team achieve their objectives.

Structure The website plan is developed using phase one information, resulting in a site map outlining main topic areas and navigational structure. The goal is to make the journey for future customers as simple as possible, ensuring an excellent user interface.

Design The design process involves carefully applying visual elements like logo and brand colors to strengthen brand identity on a website. Teams create prototypes, often clickable, to help clients feel the final look before development begins.

Build The design is typically static images, requiring extra effort to translate into HTML/CSS and add animations or JavaScript. The homepage is developed, followed by a "shell" for interior pages, which serves as the main menu. The design team populates internal pages with content, including interactive contact forms and e-commerce shopping carts.

Testing The testing process begins after content and visuals are established, ensuring all links function, website display is correct across browsers, and title and description order impacts search engine performance.

Launch After final client approval, the website should be thoroughly run-tested to ensure correct uploads, functionality, and registration of domain name and web hosting accounts.
